The carriage ride was a journey into perpetual twilight. As they crossed the border into the realm of Nocturne, the vibrant greens and golds of Elara's home faded into deep purples, indigos, and midnight blues.

The Lord of Night sat opposite her, silent as a statue. He hadn't spoken a word since the Selection. Elara clutched her small satchel, her knuckles white. She was terrified, yet a strange curiosity bloomed within her.

Why her? Why choose the only girl in the kingdom entirely devoid of magic?

"You are trembling," he said suddenly. His voice, spoken aloud this time, was deep and surprisingly gentle, lacking the oppressive mental weight from before.

***

Elara stiffened. "I am cold, My Lord."

It was a half-truth. The air in Nocturne was crisp and carried the scent of night-blooming jasmine and ancient stone.

Kaelen reached out a gloved hand and pulled a thick, fur-lined cloak from the seat beside him, offering it to her. The movement was slow, deliberate, as if he were trying not to startle a wild animal.

"We are nearly there," he said softly. "Do not fear the shadows, Elara. They only bite if you run from them."

She took the cloak, her fingers briefly brushing against the smooth leather of his glove. It was freezing to the touch. As she wrapped the heavy fur around herself, she looked out the window. Looming in the distance, bathed in the ethereal glow of a massive, silver moon, was Obsidian Keep. Her new home.
