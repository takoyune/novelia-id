Obsidian Keep was a labyrinth of dark stone corridors and grand halls lit by perpetually glowing crystals that cast a soft, blue luminescence. Elara was given a suite of rooms that rivaled the Royal Palace in luxury, though the color palette was decidedly muted.

Days turned into weeks. She rarely saw Kaelen. He was a phantom in his own castle, appearing only occasionally for silent dinners at the opposite end of a long, polished obsidian table.

He never removed his silver mask.

One evening, unable to sleep, Elara wandered the halls. The castle was quiet, the only sound the soft rustle of her skirts against the stone. She found herself drawn to a faint, rhythmic tapping sound coming from a partially open door.

***

Peeking inside, she gasped. It was a vast library, the shelves stretching up into the darkness. In the center, surrounded by towering stacks of ancient tomes, sat Kaelen.

He was slumped over a desk, clutching his head. The shadows around him were frantic, whipping and thrashing like agitated snakes. And the mask... it was off.

Elara saw his face. It was undeniably handsome, but marred by dark, pulsing veins of black energy that crept up his neck and across his cheek. He looked to be in immense pain.

Before she could retreat, a floorboard creaked beneath her foot. The shadows instantly stilled, and Kaelen's head snapped up, his piercing silver eyes locking onto hers. 

"You shouldn't be here," he growled, the shadows surging toward her.
