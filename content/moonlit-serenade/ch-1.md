The Grand Hall of Sunspear Palace was bathed in the blinding light of the midday sun, a stark contrast to the cold dread settling in Elara's stomach. Hundreds of girls, all adorned in fine silks and shimmering with innate magical auras, stood in tense silence.

Elara stood near the back, wearing a simple linen dress, feeling entirely out of place. She possessed no aura. No spark of elemental affinity. In a kingdom that valued magical prowess above all else, she was an anomaly. A blank slate.

Today was the Selection. The day the dreaded Lord of Nocturne would choose his bride, a tribute demanded every century to maintain the fragile peace between the realms of Sun and Night.

"He arrives," murmured a noblewoman near the front, her voice trembling.

***

The massive golden doors swung open, not with a creak, but with a sudden, suffocating silence that seemed to swallow the light in the room. A figure stepped through, cloaked in shadows that seemed to writhe and dance of their own accord.

Lord Kaelen. 

He was impossibly tall, his features obscured by a silver mask that reflected only the terrified faces of the crowd. He walked down the aisle, his gaze sweeping over the assembled girls. Most shrank back, their magical auras flaring in defensive panic.

He stopped halfway down the hall. He didn't look at the princesses with their fiery magic, nor the duchesses with their mastery over water. 

His head turned, his hidden gaze locking directly onto Elara.

"Her," a voice echoed, cold and smooth like polished obsidian. It didn't come from his mouth, but resonated directly in the minds of everyone present. "The one with no spark."
